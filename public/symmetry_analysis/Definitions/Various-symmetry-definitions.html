                Various symmetry definitions

                Aug 30, 20241 min read

                • symmetries
                • definitions

                In Olver’s book 1 one encounters a fair few different notions of symmetries of equations. In this note we are going to overview a few of them briefly, and finally compare them against each other.

                Throughout this note we let M=X×U be the space of independent and dependent variables.

                The first definition we will consider is the one concerning group actions on functions.

                Definition (Symmetry group)

                A symmetry group of a system S of differential equations is a local group of transformations G acting on an open subset of M such that whenever u=f(x) is a solution of S, and assuming the action g⋅f is defined for g∈G, then u~=g⋅f(x) is another solution to the system S.

                This definition is relatively straight-forward save for the intricacies of local group of transformations, which unfortunately also restricts what kind of solutions are permissible to the system S.

                Footnotes

                1. P. Olver, Applications of Lie Groups to Differential Equations. Springer New York, 1993. ↩
